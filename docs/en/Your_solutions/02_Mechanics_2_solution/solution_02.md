# Physics Solution: Harmonic Motion Analysis

## Problem Parameters
- **Mass ($m$):** $10 \text{ kg}$
- **Equation:** $x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)$

## 1. Determining the Spring Constant ($k$)
From the SHM equation $x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)$, we identify:
- **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
- **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** $10\pi \text{ rad/s}$

The relationship between $\omega$, $m$, and $k$ is:
$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \implies k = m\omega^2$$

**Calculation:**
$$k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2$$
$$k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2$$
$$k = 1000\pi^2 \approx 9869.6 \text{ N/m}$$

---

## 2. Total Mechanical Energy ($E$)
The total energy in a simple harmonic oscillator is given by the maximum potential energy stored in the spring:
$$E_{total} = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$$

**Calculation:**
$$E = \frac{1}{2} (1000\pi^2) \cdot (0.2)^2$$
$$E = 500\pi^2 \cdot 0.04$$
$$E = 20\pi^2 \approx 197.39 \text{ J}$$

**Final Results:**
- **Spring Constant ($k$):** $1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$
- **Total Energy ($E$):** $20\pi^2 \text{ J}$
