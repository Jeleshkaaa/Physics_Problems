# Physics Solution: Gravitational Dependence of a Pendulum

## 1. Period on the Moon
**Problem:** A simple pendulum has a period of 4 seconds on Earth. What is its period on the Moon where $g_M = \frac{1}{6}g_E$?

### Formula
The period is defined as:
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

### Solution
Since the length $L$ is constant, we use the proportional relationship:
$$T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}}$$

Setting up the ratio:
$$\frac{T_{Moon}}{T_{Earth}} = \sqrt{\frac{g_{Earth}}{g_{Moon}}}$$

Substituting the Moon's gravity ($\frac{1}{6}g$):
$$T_{Moon} = 4 \times \sqrt{6}$$
$$T_{Moon} \approx 9.80 \text{ seconds}$$

## 2. Required Length for a 1s Period
**Problem:** What is the required length of a simple pendulum to have a period of exactly 1 second on Earth?

### Solution
We rearrange the period formula to solve for $L$:
$$L = \frac{T^2 \cdot g}{4\pi^2}$$

Using $T = 1\text{s}$ and $g = 9.81\text{m/s}^2$:
$$L = \frac{1^2 \cdot 9.81}{4\pi^2}$$
$$L \approx 0.248\text{ meters}$$

**Result:** The pendulum must be approximately **24.8 cm** long.
