# Physics Solution: Relative Velocity and River Crossing

## Problem Statement
- **River Velocity ($V_r$):** $2\,m/s$ [East]
- **Boat Speed ($V_{br}$):** $5\,m/s$ [Still Water]
- **River Width ($W$):** $200\,m$
- **Goal:** Cross directly North.

## 1. Directional Analysis
To move directly North, the boat must head at an angle $\theta$ West of North to cancel the river's drift.

### Formula
$$\sin\theta = \frac{V_{river}}{V_{boat\_still}}$$

### Calculation
$$\sin\theta = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58^\circ$$

**Result:** The boat must head **23.6° West of North**.

---

## 2. Resultant Speed and Time
The speed at which the boat actually crosses the river (the Northward component) is found via the Pythagorean theorem.

### Resultant Velocity ($V_b$)
$$V_b = \sqrt{V_{br}^2 - V_r^2}$$
$$V_b = \sqrt{5^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58\,m/s$$

### Time to Cross ($t$)
$$t = \frac{\text{Width}}{V_b}$$
$$t = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}} \approx 43.64\,s$$

**Result:** It will take approximately **43.6 seconds** to cross the river.
