# Physics Problem: Parallel-Plate Capacitor Calculations
---

## 1. Given Information
* **Area ($S$):** $0.02 \text{ m}^2$
* **Distance ($d$):** $5 \text{ mm} = 0.005 \text{ m}$
* **Voltage ($V$):** $500 \text{ V}$
* **Permittivity of vacuum ($\epsilon_0$):** $8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ F/m}$

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Solutions

### **Task 1: Calculate the Capacitance ($C$)**
The capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor depends on the geometry and the permittivity of the medium between the plates.

**Formula:**
$$C = \frac{\epsilon_0 S}{d}$$

**Calculation:**
$$C = \frac{8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 0.02}{0.005}$$
$$C = 3.5416 \times 10^{-11} \text{ F}$$
**Result:** $\approx \mathbf{35.4 \text{ pF}}$

---

### **Task 2: Calculate the Energy Stored ($W$)**
Energy is stored in the electric field created between the charged plates.

**Formula:**
$$W = \frac{1}{2} C V^2$$

**Calculation:**
$$W = 0.5 \times (3.5416 \times 10^{-11}) \times (500)^2$$
$$W = 0.5 \times 3.5416 \times 10^{-11} \times 250,000$$
$$W = 4.427 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}$$
**Result:** $\approx \mathbf{4.43 \, \mu\text{J}}$

---

### **Task 3: Calculate the Electric Field Intensity ($E$)**
The electric field between two large parallel plates is uniform.

**Formula:**
$$E = \frac{V}{d}$$

**Calculation:**
$$E = \frac{500}{0.005}$$
$$E = 100,000 \text{ V/m}$$
**Result:** $\mathbf{10^5 \text{ V/m}}$ (or $100 \text{ kV/m}$)

---

### **Task 4: Calculate the Force of Attraction ($F$)**
The plates experience a force of attraction due to the opposite charges residing on them.

**Formula:**
$$F = \frac{\epsilon_0 S E^2}{2}$$

**Calculation:**
$$F = \frac{8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 0.02 \times (10^5)^2}{2}$$
$$F = \frac{8.854 \times 10^{-12} \times 0.02 \times 10^{10}}{2}$$
$$F = 8.854 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}$$
**Result:** $\mathbf{0.885 \text{ mN}}$

---

| Electric Field | $E$ | $100 \text{ kV/m}$ |
| Force of Attraction | $F$ | $0.885 \text{ mN}$ |
