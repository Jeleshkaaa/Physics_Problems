# Physics Problem: Ampere's Law - Magnetic Field Between Two Wires
---

## 1. Identify the Given Information
* **Current ($I_1$ and $I_2$):** Both are **5 A**, but flowing in **opposite directions**.
* **Distance between wires ($d$):** 10 cm ($0.1$ m).
* **Point of Interest:** Midway between the wires, meaning the distance ($r$) from each wire to the point is:
    $$r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{10\text{ cm}}{2} = 5\text{ cm} = 0.05\text{ m}$$

---

## 2. Determine the Direction (Right-Hand Rule)
This is the most critical step. Even though the currents are in opposite directions, their magnetic fields at the midway point actually **add together**.

* **Wire 1 (Current Up):** Point your right thumb up. Your fingers curl **"into"** the page on the right side (where the midway point is).
* **Wire 2 (Current Down):** Point your right thumb down. Since this wire is on the right, your fingers curl **"into"** the page on its left side (where the midway point is).

Because both magnetic fields ($B_1$ and $B_2$) point in the same direction (into the page), the total magnetic field ($B_{total}$) is the sum of the two.

---

## 3. Apply the Formula
The magnitude of the magnetic field $B$ at a distance $r$ from a long straight wire is given by:

$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

**Where:**
* $\mu_0$ (permeability of free space) $= 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$
* $I = 5\text{ A}$
* $r = 0.05\text{ m}$

### Calculate $B$ for one wire:
$$B_1 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7}) \times 5}{2\pi \times 0.05}$$
$$B_1 = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{0.05}$$
$$B_1 = \frac{10 \times 10^{-7}}{0.05} = 2 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$

---

## 4. Calculate the Total Magnitude
Since both wires contribute the same magnitude in the same direction:

$$B_{total} = B_1 + B_2$$
$$B_{total} = 2 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T} + 2 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$
$$B_{total} = 4 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$$

> **Note:** This can also be expressed as **$40 \mu\text{T}$** or **$0.4\text{ Gauss}$**.

---

## Final Answer
* **Magnitude:** $4 \times 10^{-5}\text{ T}$
* **Direction:** Perpendicular to the plane of the wires (directed **into the page** at the midpoint).
