# 6. Instrument Precision

**Problem Statement:**
A digital thermometer reads $25.4^\circ\text{C}$. Assuming the uncertainty is half the value of the last digit, what is the absolute uncertainty of this measurement?

---

### Solution

To find the absolute uncertainty of the measurement, we determine the resolution of the digital instrument based on its last displayed digit:

1. **Identify the place value of the last digit:** In the measurement $25.4^\circ\text{C}$, the last digit ($4$) is in the tenths place. This means the smallest increment or resolution of the thermometer is $0.1^\circ\text{C}$.

2. **Calculate the uncertainty:** The problem states that the uncertainty is half the value of this last digit's place value (resolution):
   
   $$\text{Absolute Uncertainty} = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.1^\circ\text{C} = 0.05^\circ\text{C}$$

### Final Answer
The absolute uncertainty of this measurement is **$\pm 0.05^\circ\text{C}$**.
